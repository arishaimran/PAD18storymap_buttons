A conceptual framework representing linear trends in δ18O-δ2H of prevailing meterological conditions and indivudal water budget components (i.e.,δSSL, δ*). An isotope framework is also necessary when estimating evaporation-to-inflow ratios using the dual-isotope tracer approach6,11.
A linear trajectroy offset from the Local Meteoric Water Line and primarily controlled by local atmospheric conditions (relative humidity, temperature, and the isotope composition of atmospheric mositure). The position of a lake relative to the LEL provides information about the water balance of the lake6.
The isotope composition of a lake at steady-state (i.e., evaporation = inflow). δSSL can be estimated using the equation of Gonfiantini (1986)12, which is based on the linear resistance model of Craig and Gordon (1965)13. Alternatively, an 'index lake', which is a naturally occuring terminal basin where evaporation = inflow, can be used to serve as δSSL14.
The limiting non-steady state isotope composition of a lake approaching dessication. δ* is dependent on atmospheric conditions (relative humidity, temeperature, and the isotope composition of atmospheric vapour). Theoretically, the isotope composition of all lakes experiencing similar atmospheric conditions will converege towards this point6.
